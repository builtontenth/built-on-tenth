If you're running Google Ads for your HVAC company and not running Local Service Ads, you're paying to appear below the contractors who are.

LSAs sit at the top of Google search results -- above the regular paid ads, above the map pack. They show your company name, star rating, review count, and a Google Guaranteed badge. They charge per lead, not per click. The leads are exclusive to you.

In most HVAC markets, the top three LSA positions capture 30 to 45% of clicks on the entire results page. Everything below them -- including your Google Ads -- is competing for what's left.

The reason most HVAC companies aren't running LSAs isn't that they don't know about them. It's that the setup process involves a verification step that takes time and feels bureaucratic. Most companies start it, hit a friction point, and let it sit.

This article walks through the full setup process, explains how Google ranks LSAs once you're live, and covers what to do to actually get jobs from them rather than just impressions.

---

## What LSAs Are (and What They Are Not)

**What they are:**

Local Service Ads are a Google product where you pay per lead, not per click. A lead is a call or message from a homeowner who found your LSA listing. Google shows your ad when someone searches for a service you offer in your area. You set a weekly budget. Google controls how often you appear within that budget based on your ranking signals.

The "Google Guaranteed" badge on your listing signals to homeowners that Google has verified your license, insurance, and background check. It's a trust signal that no regular ad or organic listing carries.

**What they are not:**

LSAs are not Google Ads. They run on a completely separate platform with separate billing, separate setup, and a separate ranking algorithm. Running Google Ads does not give you LSA placement. You have to set them up independently.

LSAs are also not a set-it-and-forget-it system. How you respond to leads, how you dispute bad leads, and how you manage your review profile all affect how often Google shows your ad. The mechanics are covered below.

## The Verification Process: What to Expect

This is where most HVAC companies stall. The verification process is real work -- it's also a one-time investment that creates a durable competitive advantage because many competitors never finish it.

**What you need to complete verification:**

- A Google Business Profile for your company (must be verified and active)
- A valid business license for the states you operate in
- Proof of general liability insurance (certificate of insurance)
- Background check completion for the business owner and any technicians who will appear on jobs

**How the process works:**

Go to ads.google.com/local-services-ads to start. Create your profile, select HVAC as your service category, and enter your service area. Google will prompt you to upload your license and insurance documents. After documents are submitted, Google initiates a background check through a third-party provider -- typically Evident or a similar service. The background check requires the business owner to submit a consent form.

**Realistic timeline:**

Document review: 3 to 7 business days. Background check: 5 to 10 business days. Total: 2 to 4 weeks in most cases. During peak periods (spring and fall when HVAC searches spike), verification can take longer because Google's review queue grows.

**What causes delays:**

The most common delay is mismatched business information. If your business name on your license reads "Johnson HVAC LLC" and your Google Business Profile reads "Johnson Heating and Cooling," Google flags the discrepancy. Match your GBP name, license, and insurance certificate exactly before submitting. That one step prevents most verification holds.

If your license has expired or is registered in only some of the zip codes you serve, document that clearly. Google will verify against the scope on the license.

## Setting Up Your Profile for Maximum Visibility

Once verified, the setup decisions you make directly affect how often Google shows your ad and at what position.

**Service categories:**

Select every service you actually perform, not just your primary one. AC repair, furnace repair, HVAC installation, duct cleaning, thermostat installation -- each category is eligible to trigger your ad for different search terms. Companies that select only "HVAC" miss searches for "furnace tune-up" or "AC maintenance" that would have triggered their listing.

Do not select services you don't offer or can't staff. Google penalizes dispute rates -- if you receive a lead for duct cleaning and you don't offer it, you'll dispute the lead, and high dispute rates hurt your ranking.

**Service area:**

Set your service area at the zip code level, not just the city. Google uses zip codes to match your ad to searches. If you serve 22 zip codes but only enter your primary city, you're invisible in the surrounding zip codes even though you'd take those calls.

Do not over-extend your service area to zip codes where you don't want to drive. A lead from 45 minutes outside your normal range that you dispute or don't respond to quickly hurts your ranking for all the leads you actually want.

**Business hours:**

Set your hours to match when your phones are actually staffed. If Google sends you a lead at 8pm and your phone goes unanswered, that counts against your response rate. Either set hours to your actual staffed window or set up after-hours coverage before expanding your availability. A 9pm lead you miss is worse than a 9pm lead you never received.

**Budget:**

Start with a weekly budget that covers 10 to 15 leads at your market's average lead cost. In most HVAC markets that's $800 to $1,500/week. The goal in the first 60 days is to build ranking signals -- review velocity, response rate, low dispute rate -- not to maximize volume. You can scale budget once those signals are healthy.

## How Google Ranks LSAs

Once you're live, Google decides how often to show your ad and in what position. The ranking algorithm is not fully published, but the signals that move it are well understood from how top-performing companies operate.

**Review count and rating:**

This is the strongest controllable signal. Google favors LSA profiles with a high number of recent reviews at 4.5 stars or above. The reviews that count toward LSA ranking are your Google Business Profile reviews -- not a separate pool. This means your GBP review velocity directly affects your LSA position.

A company with 45 reviews adding 10 per month will outrank a company with 120 reviews adding 1 per month within a few months. Review velocity matters more than total count once you're above roughly 30 reviews.

**Response rate and speed:**

Google tracks how quickly you respond to leads and what percentage you respond to. Responding to a lead within 15 seconds -- by answering the call when it comes in -- is the benchmark. A response rate below 80% signals to Google that you're not reliably available, and your ad frequency drops.

This connects directly to your phone operations. The [phone revenue calculator](/tools/phone-calculator) shows what your current answer rate costs per month in missed revenue -- the same dynamic applies here. Every LSA lead that rings to voicemail is a response-rate penalty paid twice: once in the missed job and once in reduced ad frequency going forward.

**Dispute rate:**

You can dispute leads that are wrong number, out of service area, duplicate, or for a service you don't offer. Disputing legitimate leads -- because you didn't want that job type, or because you didn't respond fast enough -- trains Google's algorithm that your ad is mismatch-heavy. Dispute only leads that genuinely qualify for a credit.

**Job history through Google:**

As you book and complete jobs through your LSA dashboard, Google builds a signal around your conversion history. HVAC companies that consistently turn LSA leads into booked jobs get shown more. This is Google's equivalent of campaign-level quality score -- the platform rewards ads that convert.

## What to Do With a Lead When It Comes In

An LSA lead is a phone call or a message. What happens in the first five minutes determines whether it becomes a booked job.

**For calls:**

Answer it. Answer rate is both a ranking signal and a conversion reality. An LSA lead is a homeowner who searched with high intent, saw your Google Guaranteed badge, and chose to call you specifically. The contact rate on an answered LSA call is close to 100% -- you already have them. The only way to lose it is to not pick up.

If your CSR answers, they should treat it identically to any other inbound call: confirm the issue, confirm the service area, make the booking ask. "Are mornings or afternoons better for you tomorrow?" The scripts that work for Google Ads calls work for LSA calls. The difference is you paid for the lead, not the click -- so a missed booking is a more direct cash loss.

**For messages:**

Respond within 5 minutes during business hours. Google's platform shows homeowners your typical response time, and they use it to decide whether to wait or call someone else. A message that sits for two hours is a lead you paid for that went cold before you responded.

Reply with a direct question that moves toward scheduling: "Thanks for reaching out -- what's going on with your system? We have availability tomorrow if you want to get it looked at."

## The One Setting Most Companies Miss

Inside the LSA dashboard, there is a setting called **lead preferences** that lets you specify what types of jobs you want. Most HVAC companies leave this on the default, which is every job type in every selected category.

The problem: if you're a replacement-focused company, you're receiving and paying for leads from homeowners who want a $150 tune-up. If you're a service-and-repair shop, you're receiving leads for new installs you can't staff. Both scenarios inflate your dispute rate and reduce your conversion rate, which damages ranking over time.

Go into lead preferences and deselect job types that don't fit your business model. If you don't run maintenance agreements, deselect maintenance. If you're booked out on installs for six weeks, pause new installation leads temporarily rather than receiving them and not converting. This is not a commonly documented optimization, and most competitors haven't done it. It's a free ranking improvement that takes ten minutes.

Most HVAC companies ask this question when they're allocating a fixed budget. Here's how to think about it at different stages.

| Stage | Priority order | Reasoning |

## How to Tell If Your LSAs Are Actually Working

Most HVAC owners check their LSA dashboard, see lead volume, and assume that means it's working. Lead volume is not the metric. **Cost per booked job is the metric.**

Track these four numbers monthly:

**Leads received:** Total calls and messages that came through your LSA listing.

**Leads contacted:** Of those leads, how many did you actually reach and speak with? This reveals your response rate gap.

**Jobs booked:** Of the leads you contacted, how many became scheduled appointments? This is your LSA booking rate.

**Cost per booked job:** Total LSA spend divided by jobs booked. This is the number you compare against your threshold. For most HVAC companies, a healthy LSA cost per booked job is $95 to $185. Above $250, something in the chain -- response rate, booking rate, or lead quality -- needs fixing.

If your cost per booked job is high, pull the chain apart before adjusting budget. High dispute rate means your lead preferences need tightening. Low contact rate means your response time is too slow. Low booking rate means your CSR needs the [booking ask training](/insights/why-hvac-csrs-are-losing-you-300k-per-year) before more leads will help.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much do Google Local Service Ads cost for HVAC companies?

LSAs charge per lead, not per click. In most US markets, HVAC leads through LSAs cost $65 to $130 per lead. Cost per booked job -- the number that actually matters -- runs $95 to $185 for top-performing HVAC companies with strong response rates and booking rates. Cost per lead has increased over the past two years as more contractors joined the platform, but it still produces the lowest cost per booked job of any paid HVAC channel in most markets.

### How long does Google LSA verification take for HVAC companies?

Two to four weeks in most cases. Document review takes 3 to 7 business days. Background check takes 5 to 10 business days. Delays are most common when business names don't match across license, insurance, and Google Business Profile. Match those exactly before submitting to avoid holds.

### Do I need a Google Business Profile to run LSAs?

Yes. Your GBP must be verified and active before you can run LSAs. Your GBP reviews also feed directly into your LSA ranking -- the review count and star rating on your LSA listing come from your GBP. If your GBP is thin on reviews, building review velocity before launching LSAs will improve your ranking from day one.

### Can I run LSAs and Google Ads at the same time?

Yes, and most HVAC companies at $1M+ revenue should. LSAs and Google Ads run independently and capture different segments of the search results page. LSAs capture the highest-intent clicks at the top of the page. Google Ads capture searches that don't trigger LSA placements, specific equipment keywords, and longer-tail service terms. Running both gives you more coverage without paying twice for the same searcher -- they're shown in different positions.

### What is the Google Guaranteed badge and does it matter?

The Google Guaranteed badge is a trust signal Google places on verified LSA listings. It means Google has checked the business license, insurance, and run a background check. It also means Google will back certain claims up to a limit if a customer is dissatisfied. For homeowners comparing three HVAC companies on a search results page, the badge is a visible differentiator -- especially against smaller operators who haven't completed verification. In competitive markets where two or three companies are running LSAs, the badge alone doesn't win the call. Your review count and rating do most of the work once you're in the LSA section.

### Why aren't my LSAs showing up even though I'm verified?

The four most common reasons: budget is too low to compete in your market, your review count is significantly below competitors in the LSA section, your response rate has dropped below Google's threshold, or your service area is set too narrowly and doesn't match the zip codes where homeowners are searching. Check all four before assuming there's a technical problem. Response rate is the most commonly overlooked -- if your phones aren't being answered during your listed hours, Google will throttle your ad frequency before you notice the pattern.

### How do I dispute a bad LSA lead?

Inside your LSA dashboard, go to the lead in question and select "Dispute lead." You can dispute leads that are wrong number, duplicate contacts, outside your service area, for a service type you don't offer, or from a homeowner who only wanted a quote with no intent to book. Google reviews disputes and credits your account if the lead qualifies. Dispute only legitimate cases -- high dispute rates reduce your ranking. Do not dispute leads simply because they didn't book or because the homeowner was difficult.

### What's the difference between LSA ranking and Google Ads ranking?

Google Ads ranking is determined primarily by your bid amount and Quality Score (click-through rate, landing page relevance, expected conversion rate). LSA ranking is determined by review count and velocity, response rate, dispute rate, job history, and proximity. You cannot buy your way to the top of LSAs the way you can with Google Ads bidding. The ranking is earned through operational signals, not spend. That's why it's harder to get right and harder for competitors to displace once you're there.
