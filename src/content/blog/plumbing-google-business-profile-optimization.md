When a homeowner's pipe bursts at 7pm on a Tuesday, they do not open Facebook. They do not ask a neighbor. They pick up their phone, type "emergency plumber near me," and call the first result that looks legitimate.

That first result is almost always the map pack.

The three businesses shown under the Google map get the overwhelming majority of clicks for emergency searches. The fourth result might as well be invisible. For a plumbing company, this is the most important piece of digital real estate available -- and it costs nothing to appear there except the work of setting up and maintaining the profile correctly.

Most plumbing companies have a Google Business Profile. Most of them are set up poorly, updated sporadically, and producing a fraction of the calls they should. A plumbing contractor who fixed this in six months secured 3x more booked jobs by adding fresh photos, optimizing categories, and requesting reviews after every service call, according to documented results across plumbing and HVAC contractors tracked by Plumbing & HVAC SEO.

## Why GBP Matters More for Plumbing Than Any Other Channel

Emergency plumbing calls are the highest-margin calls in the business. Gross margins on emergency service run 60 to 70% because customers prioritize speed over price comparison -- they're not getting three quotes when water is coming through their ceiling.

They're also the least expensive calls to generate. A homeowner who finds you through the map pack costs you $5 to $25 per lead. The same homeowner reached through Google Ads costs $95 to $200+. The map pack is giving you your most profitable call type at your lowest acquisition cost.

Most plumbing companies spend more money and attention on paid channels than on the free channel that produces emergency calls at one-tenth the cost. That's the imbalance this article is designed to fix.

According to Google data, businesses with complete Google Business Profiles are **70% more likely to attract local visits** than incomplete profiles. Businesses appearing in the local pack receive **93% of all clicks** from local searches -- meaning the fourth position captures roughly 7% of what position one through three shares. The math on getting into the top three versus sitting at four is not incremental. It's the difference between meaningful call volume and being ignored.

## The Four Things Google Weighs for Plumber Rankings

Google determines map pack position using three primary factors: relevance, distance, and prominence. For plumbing companies, here is what each actually means in practice.

**Relevance** is how closely your profile matches what the homeowner searched for. "Emergency plumber near me" should trigger your profile if your primary category is set correctly and your services, description, and website all signal emergency plumbing work. If your GBP says "plumbing contractor" as the only category and your description doesn't mention emergency service, Google has weaker signals to show you for urgent searches even if you offer 24/7 service.

**Distance** is how close your business address or service area is to the person searching. You can't change proximity to the searcher -- but you can make sure your service area is configured accurately so you're eligible for searches across your full coverage zone, not just the immediate neighborhood of your business address.

**Prominence** is how established and trusted your business appears based on reviews, citations, website authority, and GBP activity. This is the most controllable factor and the one where most plumbing companies have the most room to improve.

A fourth signal that's grown in importance: **behavioral engagement**. How often homeowners click your listing, call from it, and request directions tells Google whether real people find you relevant. A profile that ranks but generates no engagement gradually loses position. A profile that generates high click-through and call rates reinforces its ranking. The setup decisions below directly affect your engagement rate.

## Step 1: Categories -- The Most Important Setup Decision

Your primary category is the single most important field in your GBP. It tells Google what type of business you are and which searches you're eligible to appear for.

For almost every plumbing company, the primary category should be **"Plumber."** Not "Plumbing Contractor," not "Plumbing Supply Store" -- "Plumber." That is the category Google maps to the searches homeowners actually use.

Secondary categories let you expand your eligibility for more specific searches. Add every category that accurately reflects the work you do:

- Drain Cleaning Service
- Emergency Plumbing Service
- Water Heater Repair Service
- Water Softener Supplier (if applicable)
- Sewer Repair Service (if applicable)

Do not add categories for services you don't offer. Google can penalize profiles that receive contacts for services they don't provide -- a form of category mismatch that hurts your relevance score.

The categories field is the most common setup mistake among plumbing companies. Many have only one category. The secondary categories are where you capture searches for specific services rather than just "plumber near me."

## Step 2: Service Area -- Don't Leave Zip Codes Uncovered

Plumbing companies are service area businesses -- you travel to customers rather than having them come to you. This means you should configure your profile with a service area rather than (or in addition to) a street address.

Set your service area at the zip code or city level to match where you actually want to receive calls. Most plumbing companies set it too narrowly and cut themselves out of adjacent neighborhoods where they'd happily take jobs.

The error most commonly seen: a plumber sets their service area to their primary city and doesn't include the surrounding suburbs and towns they service daily. They rank in their city and are invisible in the adjacent areas that represent 30 to 40% of their potential call volume.

Go through your last 90 days of jobs and list every zip code or city where you completed work. Add all of them. You can include up to 20 service areas in your GBP. Use them.

**One important note on address:** If you operate from a home address you'd rather not publish, you can hide the address and show only your service area. Google still uses your location to factor proximity into rankings -- hiding the address doesn't remove the proximity signal, it just prevents the address from appearing publicly.

## Step 3: Business Description -- Signal Emergency Availability Directly

Your business description is 750 characters to tell Google and homeowners exactly what you do, where you do it, and when you're available.

Most plumbing company descriptions are generic: "We are a full-service plumbing company dedicated to quality and customer satisfaction." That tells Google almost nothing specific about your services or your market.

A description that actually works includes:

- The services you most want to rank for ("emergency plumbing, drain cleaning, water heater installation, leak detection")
- The service area ("serving [City] and surrounding [County/Region]")
- Availability signal ("available 24/7 for emergency calls" if true)
- One trust signal ("licensed master plumbers since [year]" or "flat-rate pricing with upfront quotes")

Example of a functional description:

> "Emergency plumbing services available 24/7 in [City] and [County]. Licensed and insured plumbers handling burst pipes, drain cleaning, water heater repair and installation, leak detection, and full repipes. Flat-rate pricing -- we quote the job before we start. Serving [City], [City], and [City] for over [X] years."

That description uses service language homeowners search for, names the service area, signals emergency availability, and includes a differentiator. It takes five minutes to write and most plumbing companies don't have it.

## Step 4: Hours -- Emergency Availability Is a Ranking Signal

Your listed hours affect two things: which searches you appear for and your response rate score.

If your listed hours are 8am to 5pm and a homeowner searches for an emergency plumber at 9pm, Google has weak reason to show you -- your profile signals you're closed.

Two options for plumbing companies that do take after-hours emergency calls:

**Option 1:** List extended hours that match your actual answering capability. If you or an answering service picks up calls until 9pm, list hours until 9pm.

**Option 2:** Add specific emergency hours as a separate attribute. GBP allows "emergency hours" as a distinct attribute -- this signals availability beyond your primary hours for urgent searches without misrepresenting your regular service windows.

Do not list "Open 24 hours" if you don't have someone answering at 3am. Google's algorithm tracks response rates -- profiles that list 24/7 hours but consistently miss calls outside business hours see their ranking penalize over time. The gap between listed availability and actual response behavior is a negative signal.

## Step 5: Photos -- Frequency Beats Quality

Plumbing company GBP photos serve two purposes: they signal to Google that the profile is actively managed, and they help homeowners decide whether to call before they've read a review.

GBP profiles with 100 or more photos receive significantly more calls than those with fewer images, according to local SEO benchmarks from Roofing SEO Guy (applicable across home service trades). The threshold matters less than the consistency -- profiles adding two to three new photos per month continuously perform better over time than profiles with 50 photos added at once and nothing since.

What photos to add:

- Trucks with visible company logo and phone number
- Techs in uniform on jobs (with homeowner permission)
- Before-and-after shots of completed work -- drain clearing, water heater installations, repipe jobs
- Any certifications or licensing documentation that's visually presentable

What not to add: stock images, random internet photos, or anything staged to look like work you didn't do. Google's systems have become increasingly good at identifying inauthentic visual content, and fake photos are a suspension risk.

The practical system: every tech takes one photo at the beginning of each job (the problem) and one at the end (the resolution). Someone in the office uploads two to three per week from that pool. Within 60 days, your photo count is competitive with most markets.

## Step 6: Google Posts -- One Per Week, Specific Topics

Google Posts appear on your GBP in search results and signal active profile management. The algorithm rewards profiles that post regularly with modestly higher relevance scores over time -- the cumulative effect of consistent activity matters more than any single post.

The content doesn't need to be elaborate. One post per week covering:

- A recent job type ("installed three water heaters in [neighborhood] this week -- here's what we found")
- A seasonal service ("frozen pipe season is starting -- here's how to shut off your main water valve if you need to")
- A promotion or offer ("$50 off water heater installation booked before [date]")
- An answer to a common customer question ("how to know if you have a slab leak")

Keep posts under 150 words. Include a photo if possible. End with a call to action ("call us at [number]" or "book online at [link]"). Posts expire after seven days if you don't set a specific end date, so fresh content weekly keeps the profile appearing active.

## Reviews: The Most Important Ongoing Activity

Everything above is setup. Reviews are the ongoing operation that determines whether you maintain or lose map pack position over time.

The review dynamics for plumbing are specific: high job volume (a $1M plumbing company might run 400 to 700 service calls per year) but notoriously low ask rate. Most plumbing techs don't ask for reviews because the interaction feels transactional -- fix the thing, get paid, leave. Nobody likes asking for something at the end of that exchange.

The result: plumbing companies with years of operation sitting at 35 to 50 reviews while HVAC companies half their age have 150.

**The review system that closes this gap:**

A text from the company number within one hour of job completion with a direct link. Not an email. Not a verbal ask. A text, with a direct Google review URL (generate this from your GBP at g.page/[yourbusiness]/review).

The text:

> "Hi [name], thanks for having us out today. If we did a good job, a quick Google review means a lot -- here's the link: [URL]. Appreciate it."

That text, sent within an hour, produces a 15 to 22% review conversion rate based on observed results across home service companies. A tech completing four jobs per day and sending that text on each one generates 0.6 to 0.9 reviews per day -- 12 to 18 per month from a single technician.

Track review count by technician monthly. Share the numbers in a morning meeting. The techs who are sending the text show up clearly. The ones who aren't also show up clearly. Behavior changes when the data is visible.

For competitive plumbing markets, target 6 to 10 new reviews per month as a starting velocity. Compare your monthly add rate against the top three companies in your market pack. Your goal is to match or exceed their velocity -- not just their total count.

## The Signals Most Plumbers Ignore

Two GBP features that most plumbing companies set up once and never touch again:

**Q&A section.** Google allows anyone to ask questions on your profile, and anyone to answer them. Most profiles have unanswered questions sitting there for months. Plumbing company owners should review and answer Q&A monthly -- questions about pricing, availability, service area, licensing. Each answer is a content signal that helps Google understand your services. You can also seed the Q&A yourself: ask questions your customers commonly have and answer them. "Do you offer same-day emergency service?" answered with a detailed response adds useful content to the profile without requiring a customer to initiate it.

**Attributes.** GBP allows you to set specific attributes that appear on your listing: "24-hour emergency service," "licensed and insured," "free estimates," "Spanish-speaking staff." Plumbing customers specifically look for 24/7 service and licensure signals before calling. These attributes appear in the listing itself, visible before a homeowner clicks through to your profile. Setting "24-hour emergency service" to true when you offer it is a one-time action that improves click-through on every emergency search your profile appears for.

## How GBP and Paid Channels Work Together

The [plumbing marketing playbook](/insights/plumbing-company-marketing-playbook) established that GBP produces the lowest cost per booked job of any channel for emergency calls -- $5 to $25 per lead. LSAs produce the next best paid channel result at $55 to $110. Everything else costs more per booked job.

The practical implication: GBP optimization should always come before adding paid channels. An unoptimized GBP with two reviews at position six in the map pack loses emergency calls to competitors at zero marginal cost. Adding $2,000/month in LSA spend to a weak GBP profile is building on an unstable foundation.

The correct sequencing for most plumbing companies under $1M revenue:

1. Get GBP fully built out (categories, description, service area, photos, attributes)
2. Build review velocity to match your top competitor's monthly add rate
3. Add LSAs once you have 25+ reviews and are appearing consistently in the top five
4. Add Google Ads for planned replacement work once LSAs are running and tracked

The revenue per technician discussion in the [plumbing scaling article](/insights/revenue-per-technician-plumbing-business-scaling) notes that marketing spend produces results proportional to how efficiently the downstream operation converts calls into booked jobs. A strong GBP generates cheap, high-intent emergency calls -- but those calls still need to be answered within minutes and converted at a high rate. The phone operation and the GBP are both necessary; fixing one while ignoring the other leaves money uncollected.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I optimize my plumbing Google Business Profile for emergency calls?

Set your primary category to "Plumber" and add "Emergency Plumbing Service" as a secondary category. Include "24-hour emergency service" in your attributes if you offer it. Make sure your business description explicitly mentions emergency availability and your service area. List your hours to match when you actually answer calls. A profile that signals emergency availability in categories, attributes, description, and hours ranks more consistently for emergency searches than one that doesn't.

### How many Google reviews does a plumbing company need to rank?

The target is relative to your local competitors, not an absolute number. Check the review count of the top three companies in your map pack. Your goal is to match or exceed the monthly add rate of whoever's in position one or two. In small markets, 30 to 60 reviews may be competitive. In mid-size markets, 60 to 120. In large metros, 150+. Velocity -- how many new reviews you're adding per month -- matters more than total count once you're past an initial baseline.

### What is the most important part of a plumbing Google Business Profile?

Primary category, then reviews, then business description. Primary category determines which searches you're eligible for -- "Plumber" as the primary category is the single most important field. Reviews determine your competitive position against other eligible businesses. Business description and other content fields add supporting relevance signals but rarely overcome a weak category setup or low review velocity.

### How often should plumbing companies post on Google Business Profile?

Once per week is the target. Posts signal active profile management and contribute to relevance scores over time. Keep posts short (under 150 words), include a photo when possible, and end with a call to action. Topics can be recent job types, seasonal tips, promotions, or answers to common customer questions. Consistency matters more than post quality -- a weekly post with a phone photo beats a monthly post with professional photography.

### Does adding photos to a plumbing GBP help with rankings?

Yes, and significantly. Profiles with 100+ photos receive notably more calls than those with fewer images. Regular photo uploads (two to three per week) signal an active profile to Google's algorithm. More importantly, photos influence whether homeowners click through once your profile appears in results -- a profile with authentic job photos reads as more legitimate than one with a single stock image. Photos of trucks, techs, and completed jobs are more effective than generic office photos.

### Why isn't my plumbing company showing up in the Google map pack?

The most common causes: primary category set incorrectly (not "Plumber"), service area configured too narrowly or not at all, fewer reviews than competitors in your market, low review velocity compared to top-three companies, or profile inactivity (no recent posts or photo uploads). Check all five before assuming a technical problem. For profiles that are set up correctly but still not ranking, website signals -- mobile speed, location-specific content, consistent NAP across directories -- are the next place to investigate.

### How do I get more Google reviews for my plumbing company?

Send a direct text with a Google review link within one hour of job completion. Not an email, not a verbal ask -- a text with a direct clickable URL while the homeowner is still satisfied and the job is fresh. Track which techs are sending the text monthly. Companies that implement this consistently typically see monthly review velocity increase three to five times within 60 days. At four jobs per day per tech, one text per job produces 12 to 18 reviews per month from a single technician.
